Postdoctoral Researcher in Theoretical Quantum Physics

I am a postdoctoral researcher in theoretical quantum physics with extensive experience in mathematical and numerical modeling, optimization, and high-performance simulations. My research focuses on designing control protocols for nanoscale quantum systems.
This portfolio presents selected projects illustrating my background in computational modeling and data-driven analysis.

Optimal Control of a Levitated Quantum Particle

Designed static potentials to control a levitated nanoparticle and enhance its quantum properties. Simulated the particle’s time evolution and analyzed its quantum state with workflows for modeling, propagation, and constrained optimization.

Remote Manipulation of Qubits in Waveguides

Designed electromagnetic pulses to manipulate qubits in a waveguide using analytical modeling and simulations of time evolution. Collaborated with experimentalists, and the proposed protocol was successfully implemented in the laboratory.

Wildfire Impact and Vegetation Regeneration

A small Earth Observation project analyzing a wildfire in a chosen region of the Mediterranean. Tracked vegetation dynamics before, during, just after, and two years later using NDVI maps. The results show a clear recovery of vegetation already within two years after the event. (Implemented in Google Earth Engine JavaScript API using Sentinel-2 satellite imagery).
